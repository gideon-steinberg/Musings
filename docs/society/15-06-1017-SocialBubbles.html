Social Bubbles

I watch a lot of youtube. Youtube has a recommendations algorithm to find videos it thinks you will like based on your watch history.
I LOVE this feature of youtube. It allows me to see videos I like watching. I tend to watch the same type of videos and I think youtube knows what I like. These tend to be educational focused, with lots of facts in them. I also tend to recognise the channels I watch.
I have been trying to broaden my horizons but it is really hard to look for content when you don't know what to search for!

I have gotten into a few new things, for example blacksmithing. But that did not really last very long. It is now back to my normal scheduled programing of learning cool stuff about the world.

Then it happened. I looked at the videos in "Popular in (Area you live in)".
None of the videos were from channels I recognised. In fact I would go so far and say that NONE of the videos were what I normally watch.
This was today and I realised something, social bubbles are a real thing.

The other thing I realised was the fact that I actually did NOT want to watch any of the "popular" videos. Youtube's algorithm to find videos for me is working very well.

What is a social bubble?

As you watch content recommendation systems will find out what you like watching. These recommendation systems are in everything, youtube, netflix, and facebook.
You will eventually find yourself trapped inside a specific social space. You only see things from that space. For me that is educational content. This is the "bubble" in the social bubble.

It is very hard to break out of a social bubble!

So why do you care if you are trapped in a social bubble?

Being in a social bubble you tend to only see one side of things. For me this means I get directed to positive content that I enjoy but it can be very different.

I did not understand why until recently people did not see eye to eye in recent elections. Both sides lived in a social bubble and could not see the other sides point of view. They could not even consider why anyone would vote for the other side.
This has always been around to some degree but due to recent technology advances it is very very easy to get trapped in a social bubble.

Luckily for me the bubble I live in is not completely polical. I sometimes watch some political content and I am trying to tell youtube to stop it!
Youtube has a "not interested" feature in their recommendation system and I will probably write a further article if I can get youtube to knock it off.

I can see how that type of content makes you only see one view of the world and I have seen the light. I used to agree with one side. However I think this is because I am familiar with their views.
These are not views I want to hold and I am trying to get out of it. For me at least this only makes me sadder when I engage in this type of content.

Hopefully you can see the social bubble you are in and either accept it or try to break away. This is a real thing!
